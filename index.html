Sign up here to volunteer for Election Watch!

There are a number of programs in place to identify and report voter suppression issues on Election Day (and during the 30 - 60 days prior to Election Day) - but many of the decisions that prevent citizens from exercising their right to vote are made long before then. More than 3,100 county-based election boards all over the country, many of them operating behind closed doors and without adequate oversight, meet and make decisions year-round.

That’s why we created Election Watch.

Our goal is to match at least one volunteer lawyer with every county (or county-equivalent) in the United States, and train those volunteers to identify and report on possible voter suppression issues BEFORE it’s too late to do something about them. Issues will be prioritized and escalated with the help of our partners for this program, the Lawyers’ Committee for Civil Rights and the Voting Rights Institute, a project of the American Constitution Society.

We need YOUR help to make this program a reality. If you’re a lawyer, law student, or paralegal and you’d like to learn more about what YOU can do to defend voting rights in your community, please fill out the form below.
