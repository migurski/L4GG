The Lawyers for Good Government Foundation recently partnered with the Lawyers' Committee for Civil Rights Under Law and the American Constitution Society to launch a voting rights program, the goal of which is to ensure that local election-related decisions are tracked, monitored, and escalated when necessary to protect voting rights.

Through this program, we will match volunteer lawyers, law students, and paralegals with specific counties or voting districts, and then train volunteers to identify and report on potential issues (such as overly burdensome voter ID laws, changes to polling locations, purging registered voters from the rolls, etc).

We're also working to set up similar programs for other types of issues (such as criminal justice reform), whereby lawyers will be matched with specific geographic areas and provided with the training necessary to provide on-the-ground information that can be used as the basis for civil rights litigation, legislative advocacy, or other measures.

If you'd like to learn more, or you're ready to volunteer in your local area, please fill out the form below. Thank you for your interest and your commitment to civil rights and good government.
